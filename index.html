Dora Akunyili OFR

The Nigerian public servant who took on the fake drugs trade.

Here's a timeline of Dora Akunyili's life:

  • 1954 - Born Dora Edemobi, in Makurdi, Benue State, Nigeria.

  • 1973 - Graduates from secondary school, goes on to study Pharmacology at the University of Nigeria, on scholarship.

  • 1978 - She graduates.

  • 1981 - Becomes a Graduate Assistant in the FaU.N.N. of Pharmaceutical Sciences.

  • 1985 - Receives her Ph.D in Ethnopharmacology.

  • 1990-1996 - She becomes a Senior Lecturer and consequently was made a Consultant Pharmacologist at the College of Medicine.

  • 1996 - Becomes Zonal Secretary of the Petroleum Special Trust Fund (PTF) - coordinating projects funded by profits from oil in Nigeria's South Eastern States.

  • 2001 - Appointed as the Director General of NAFDAC by the President Olusegun Obasanjo.

  • 2001-2009 - As DG of NAFDAC, she starts a war against counterfeit drugs. The agency sensitizes the populace about the dangers of substandard drugs, confisticates thousands of pounds(£) worth of fake drugs. She secures several convictions of counterfeiters, and influenced more direct & powerful surveillance at Nigerian Customs.

  • 2008 - Appointed Minister of Information & Communications.

  • 2010 - Resigns as Minister to run for office of Senator.

  • 2012 - Her book: The War Against Counterfeit Medicine: My Story was published.

  • 2014 - Dies, after a two-year battle with uterine cancer in India. She was aged 59.

She was married to Chike Akunyili and had 6 children.

Women tend to be less corrupt and more focused. There are many more great women leaders out there who need to be given a chance - Dora Akunyili.
